## Keep Your Opal Ice Maker Sparkling Clean with a Side Tank

Are you enjoying that delightful chewable nugget ice from your Opal ice maker? It is a fantastic appliance, but like all water-using devices, it needs regular cleaning. A clean ice maker ensures your ice tastes fresh and your machine lasts longer. This guide will walk you through the precise steps to clean your Opal ice maker, especially focusing on models with the convenient side tank. I will share easy tips to keep your machine in top condition.

### Takeaway

*   Regularly clean your Opal ice maker to prevent mold and mineral buildup.
*   Use a solution of white vinegar or a GE Opal cleaning kit for descaling.
*   Always unplug the machine and empty all water before cleaning.
*   Clean the side tank, reservoir, and internal components thoroughly.
*   Perform a rinse cycle after cleaning to remove any residue.

To clean your Opal ice maker with a side tank, unplug it and drain all water. Use a descaling solution like white vinegar or a specific Opal cleaner. Run a clean cycle, then scrub accessible parts, including the side tank. Rinse all components thoroughly to remove cleaning solution residue.

Regular cleaning prevents these issues, keeping your ice maker hygienic and efficient. I always advise cleaning your Opal ice maker at least once a week or every other week. This frequency keeps it sparkling and your ice tasting great. Think of it as essential maintenance for a treasured appliance.

Regular cleaning also extends the lifespan of your ice maker. When mineral deposits accumulate, they force the machine to work harder. This extra strain can cause parts to wear out faster. A well-maintained machine runs smoothly and efficiently. This saves you money on potential repairs or replacements. Taking a few minutes to clean your Opal ice maker protects your investment.

Keeping your ice maker clean is simple. It does not require complex tools or harsh chemicals. You only need some basic household items and a little bit of time. My personal experience shows that a consistent cleaning routine makes a big difference. You will notice the improved ice quality right away. This simple task brings big benefits.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Your Opal Ice Maker

Cleaning your Opal ice maker with a side tank is a straightforward process. Following these steps ensures a thorough and effective clean. I always recommend doing this on a regular schedule. This keeps your ice maker working its best.

### 1. Preparing Your Ice Maker

First, you must prepare the machine for cleaning. Safety is important here.
*   **Unplug the Ice Maker:** Always disconnect the power cord from the outlet. This prevents any electrical hazards during cleaning. Never clean a plugged-in appliance.
*   **Drain All Water:** Remove the side tank and empty any water inside it. Also, drain the water from the main reservoir of the ice maker. The Opal usually has a drain plug or hose at the back for this. Make sure no water remains in the system. Emptying the machine prevents mixing old water with cleaning solutions.

### 2. Disassembling Removable Parts

Next, take out any parts that come off easily.
*   **Remove the Ice Bin and Drip Tray:** Take out the ice storage bin and the drip tray beneath the ice chute. These parts often collect ice chips and water. Wash them thoroughly with warm water and mild dish soap. Rinse them well and let them air dry completely.
*   **Detach the Side Tank:** Carefully remove the side tank from its connection. This tank often holds extra water, feeding the main reservoir. Clean the side tank separately for best results. You can find more specific guidance on cleaning ice maker components in general cleaning guides, such as [how to clean an ice maker](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-ice-maker).

### 3. Cleaning the Side Tank and Reservoir

The side tank and the main reservoir are critical areas to clean. They hold the water that becomes your ice.

*   **Clean the Side Tank:** Fill the side tank with a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water. Swish the solution around vigorously. Let it sit for about 15-20 minutes to loosen any deposits. Use a soft brush or sponge to scrub the inside of the tank. Pay attention to any visible film or mineral buildup. Rinse the side tank thoroughly with clean water until no vinegar smell remains. For specific models like the Opal 2.0, the side tank cleaning process is similar and crucial for overall maintenance. Refer to specialized guides like [how to clean Opal 2.0 ice maker with side tank](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-opal-20-ice-maker-with-side-tank) for model-specific tips.
*   **Clean the Main Water Reservoir:** For the internal reservoir, you will run a cleaning cycle. Fill the main reservoir with the same 50/50 white vinegar and water solution. Ensure the side tank is empty and properly reattached.

### 4. Running the Cleaning Cycle

The cleaning cycle is essential for descaling the internal parts.

*   **Initiate Clean Cycle:** Plug the ice maker back in. Press and hold the "Clean" button (or "Light" button on some models) for three seconds. The "Clean" indicator light will illuminate. The machine will begin circulating the vinegar solution through its internal components. This cycle typically lasts about 15-20 minutes. It helps break down mineral deposits on the coils and pump.
*   **Drain the Solution:** Once the cleaning cycle finishes, the "Clean" light will turn off. Unplug the machine again. Place a large basin under the drain hose at the back of the unit. Remove the drain plug and let all the vinegar solution drain completely. This removes loosened scale and the cleaning agent.

### 5. Rinsing the System

Rinsing is vital to remove all traces of the cleaning solution. You do not want vinegar-flavored ice.

*   **Perform Multiple Rinses:** Re-insert the drain plug. Fill the main water reservoir with fresh, clean water. Do not add the side tank for this step. Plug the machine back in. Press and hold the "Clean" button again to run another cycle with just clean water. This flushes out any remaining vinegar.
*   **Drain Rinse Water:** After the cycle, unplug the unit and drain the water completely as before. Repeat this rinsing process two to three more times. This ensures thorough removal of all cleaning solution residue. Your goal is to eliminate any lingering vinegar smell or taste.

### 6. Final Assembly and Operation

You are almost done. Just a few more steps.

*   **Dry and Reassemble:** Once all rinsing is complete and water is fully drained, let the interior air dry for a few hours. Ensure all removable parts, like the ice bin and side tank, are completely dry. Reassemble them back into the ice maker.
*   **Add Fresh Water:** Fill the side tank and/or main reservoir with fresh, filtered water.
*   **Start Making Ice:** Plug in your Opal ice maker. It is now ready to produce fresh, clean nugget ice. Enjoy the crisp taste.

By following these steps, you can keep your Opal ice maker in excellent condition. This ensures delightful ice for years to come.

## Deep Cleaning the Opal Side Tank and Water Reservoir

The side tank and main water reservoir of your Opal ice maker are critical components. They constantly hold water, making them prime spots for mineral buildup and potential growth of mold or mildew. A deep clean of these areas is essential. This ensures your ice is always pure and fresh. I prioritize these sections during every cleaning session.

The side tank, specifically, allows for continuous water flow, which means more opportunities for impurities. To effectively clean it, you must detach it completely. This allows you to inspect its interior. Look for any slime, discoloration, or hard water spots. These indicate mineral deposits or biological growth. If you notice any of these, apply a dedicated descaling agent or a strong solution of white vinegar directly. For guidance on cleaning various water-holding components, similar principles apply to [how to clean a water tank](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-water-tank).

For deep cleaning the side tank:

1.  **Preparation:** Remove the side tank and empty any remaining water.
2.  **Cleaning Solution:** Fill the tank with a solution of 50% white vinegar and 50% warm water. If there is significant buildup, you can use a higher concentration of vinegar, up to full strength.
3.  **Soak and Scrub:** Let the solution sit in the tank for at least 30 minutes, or even a few hours for heavy deposits. Use a soft bottle brush or a sponge attached to a handle to scrub the inside surfaces. Pay close attention to corners and seams. Mineral deposits can cling stubbornly.
4.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** After scrubbing, empty the vinegar solution. Rinse the tank repeatedly with fresh, clean water. Continue rinsing until you cannot detect any vinegar smell. This step is crucial to prevent your ice from tasting like vinegar. Allow the tank to air dry completely before reattaching it to the ice maker.

The main water reservoir, while not removable for all parts, still requires deep cleaning. The cleaning cycle with vinegar takes care of the internal components. However, you can also manually clean accessible parts. Use a damp cloth soaked in a vinegar solution to wipe down the walls of the reservoir. Reach into any visible corners or ledges. This helps remove any film that the automated cycle might miss. Some users also clean [sensors on their Opal ice maker](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-sensors-on-opal-ice-maker) to ensure proper operation, as mineral deposits can sometimes affect them.

After cleaning these water-holding areas, always run multiple rinse cycles through the main ice maker unit. This flushes out any loosened particles and ensures no cleaning solution remains. ## Maintaining Your Opal Ice Maker's Cleanliness

Keeping your Opal ice maker consistently clean requires more than just occasional deep cleans. Regular maintenance habits prevent significant buildup. This makes future cleaning sessions much easier. I have found that a consistent routine saves a lot of effort in the long run. Small steps daily or weekly make a big difference.

Here are my top tips for ongoing maintenance:

*   **Daily Draining and Drying:** If you do not use your ice maker daily, or if you will be away for a few days, always drain the water. Empty the ice bin and let the interior air dry. Stagnant water is a breeding ground for mold and mineral deposits. This simple habit prevents issues from forming.
*   **Wipe Down the Exterior:** Use a soft, damp cloth to wipe down the exterior of your Opal ice maker. Pay attention to the control panel and the area around the ice chute. This removes dust, spills, and fingerprints. A clean exterior reflects a well-cared-for appliance.
*   **Clean the Ice Bin Regularly:** Even if you empty the ice bin, residue can stick to the sides. Wash the ice bin with warm, soapy water after each thorough cleaning cycle. You can do this more frequently if you notice any stickiness or discoloration. Make sure it is completely dry before returning it to the machine.
*   **Use Filtered Water:** Tap water contains various minerals and impurities. These are the main culprits behind scale buildup. Using filtered water significantly reduces the rate of mineral accumulation. Many Opal users connect their machines to filtered water lines. This is a simple but effective preventative measure. It also improves the taste of your ice.
*   **Run a Quick Rinse Cycle:** If you notice a slight change in ice taste or appearance, run a quick rinse cycle with just clean water. This helps flush out any minor impurities before they become a major problem. It is a good interim step between full cleaning cycles.
*   **Avoid Harsh Chemicals:** Never use bleach, abrasive cleaners, or sharp objects inside your ice maker. These can damage the internal components and leave harmful residues. Stick to white vinegar or recommended cleaning solutions. For general cleaning tasks like how to clean an ice maker, vinegar is often the safest bet. If you are specifically looking for a vinegar-based solution, consider reviewing guides on [how to clean an ice maker with vinegar](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-ice-maker-with-vinegar).
*   **Check Vents and Drip Tray:** Periodically check the air vents on your machine for dust buildup. Dust can hinder proper airflow. Keep the drip tray clean and empty to prevent water from pooling, which can lead to unsightly stains and potential mold growth.

By adopting these maintenance habits, you will ensure your Opal ice maker remains in excellent working order. Your ice will consistently be fresh, clean, and delicious. Consistent care makes all the difference.

## Troubleshooting Common Opal Ice Maker Cleaning Issues

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter a few common issues with your Opal ice maker. Knowing how to troubleshoot these problems can save you time and frustration. I have come across these situations myself, and a little knowledge goes a long way. Most issues relate to insufficient cleaning or mineral buildup.

### 1. Ice Tastes Funny or Smells Bad

This is a very common complaint and almost always points to a need for deeper cleaning.
*   **Cause:** Mineral buildup, mold, or stagnant water.
*   **Solution:** Perform a thorough cleaning cycle using a strong vinegar solution. Ensure you clean the side tank and ice bin meticulously. Run at least three rinse cycles with fresh water to completely flush out residues. Sometimes, a persistent smell means mold is deeper inside. If so, a professional cleaning kit might be more effective.

### 2. Ice Production Slows Down

If your Opal is making less ice than usual, or the ice is smaller, mineral scale is likely interfering.
*   **Cause:** Scale buildup on the evaporator coils or water lines.
*   **Solution:** Run a descaling cycle. Use a higher concentration of vinegar (75% vinegar, 25% water) or a commercial descaling solution specifically designed for ice makers. The scale reduces the efficiency of the cooling elements. Regular descaling helps maintain optimal ice production. This is similar to issues found in any [table top ice maker](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-table-top-ice-maker) if not cleaned often.

### 3. "Clean" Light Stays On After Cleaning

Sometimes, the light might not turn off, indicating the machine thinks it is still dirty.
*   **Cause:** Incomplete cleaning cycle, residual solution, or sensor issue.
*   **Solution:** First, ensure you completed all drain and rinse cycles fully. If the light persists, unplug the machine for 5-10 minutes. This can reset the sensors. If the issue continues, it might be a sensor problem. You might need to inspect or clean the sensors. Guides like [how to clean sensors on Opal ice maker](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-sensors-on-opal-ice-maker) offer specific advice for this. Mineral deposits on the sensors can sometimes prevent them from working correctly.

### 4. Water Leaks from the Side Tank or Main Unit

Leaks can be alarming, but often have simple solutions.
*   **Cause:** Improperly seated side tank, cracked reservoir, or clogged drain line.
*   **Solution:** Check that the side tank is seated correctly and securely. Ensure its seals are intact and not twisted. If the leak is from the main unit, inspect the drain plug at the back; make sure it is fully inserted. A cracked reservoir or hose could be the problem. If you suspect internal damage, contact Opal customer support. Ensure no ice is blocking the drain or water path.

### 5. Mold or Pink Slime Reappears Quickly

This indicates a persistent issue, often due to high mineral content or insufficient drying.
*   **Cause:** Warm environment, high mineral water, not enough drying time.
*   **Solution:** Increase cleaning frequency. Ensure you are draining and drying the unit completely, especially when not in use. Consider using only filtered water, as minerals provide nutrients for mold. If possible, keep the ice maker in a cooler, well-ventilated area. This environment is less conducive to mold growth. For persistent mold issues, consider reviewing comprehensive guides on [how to clean mold from portable ice maker](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mold-from-portable-ice-maker).

By understanding these common issues and their solutions, you can keep your Opal ice maker running smoothly. Regular and thorough cleaning remains the best preventive measure. Do not hesitate to consult your user manual for specific model instructions if these general tips do not resolve your problem.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I clean my Opal ice maker with a side tank?
You should clean your Opal ice maker with a side tank at least once a week or every two weeks. If you live in an area with hard water, clean it more frequently. This regular schedule prevents mineral buildup and mold growth. It also ensures your ice always tastes fresh.

### Can I use bleach to clean my Opal ice maker?
No, you should never use bleach to clean your Opal ice maker. Bleach is a harsh chemical that can damage the internal components of the machine. It can also leave behind harmful residues that are unsafe for consumption. Always use white vinegar or a specifically formulated ice maker cleaning solution.

### What kind of water should I use in my Opal ice maker?
Using filtered water in your Opal ice maker is highly recommended. Tap water contains minerals that contribute to scale buildup over time. Filtered water significantly reduces this mineral accumulation. It also improves the taste and clarity of your ice.

### How do I know if my Opal ice maker needs cleaning?
Signs that your Opal ice maker needs cleaning include discolored ice, reduced ice production, a strange taste or smell in the ice, or visible slime or mold inside the machine. If the "Clean" light illuminates, it is also a direct indicator to run a cleaning cycle.

### What is the purpose of the side tank on an Opal ice maker?
The side tank on an Opal ice maker provides an extended water reservoir. It automatically feeds water into the main unit. This feature allows the ice maker to produce more ice before needing a refill. It is a convenient way to keep a continuous supply of nugget ice.
